ASSESSMENT PREVALENCE OF SUBSTANCE USE 2025

A comprehensive research project by the Department of Public Health, Horn International College, Jijiga, Ethiopia.

Explore the Study Download Full Proposal

About The Study

This research aims to assess the prevalence of substance use and identify key associated factors among students at Horn International College. Substance use is a significant global public health concern, particularly among youth, leading to adverse health, academic, and socio-economic outcomes.


Research Significance

The findings of this study will provide crucial data for healthcare practitioners and policymakers to design targeted interventions, prevention strategies, and support systems within the academic environment. It will also contribute to the body of knowledge for further research on this topic.

General Objective

To assess the prevalence of substance use and associated factors among Horn College students in Eastern Ethiopia, 2025.

Specific Objectives

  • To determine the prevalence of substance use among Horn College students
  • To identify factors associated to substance use among Horn College students

Research Methodology

Study Design

A quantitative cross-sectional study will be employed to gather comprehensive data.

Study Area & Period

Horn International College, Jigjiga, Somali Regional State. Data collection will take place in September 2025.

Sample Size

A total of 403 students will be selected using a stratified multi-stage sampling technique.

Data Collection

Data will be collected through self-administered structured questionnaires translated to the local language.
